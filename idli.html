Idli Sambhar

South India's favourite morning ritual — fluffy steamed rice cakes with tangy lentil stew.

⏱
Prep Time 15 mins
🔥
Cook Time 25 mins
📊
Difficulty Easy
👤
Serves 4 people

Ingredients

  • For Idli (using ready batter):
  • 2 cups idli batter (fermented)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil to grease moulds
  • For Sambhar:
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (drumstick, carrot, onion, tomato)
  • 2 tbsp sambhar powder
  • 1 lemon-sized ball of tamarind (soaked)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 10 curry leaves
  • 2 dried red chillies
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Cook the Dal

Wash toor dal and pressure cook with 2 cups water and turmeric for 3–4 whistles until completely soft. Once pressure releases, whisk the dal to a smooth, lump-free consistency. Set aside.

2

Prepare the Tamarind Water

Soak tamarind in ½ cup warm water for 10 minutes. Squeeze and strain to get thick tamarind pulp. Discard the fibrous residue. This tangy extract is the soul of a good sambhar.

3

Cook the Vegetables

In a wide pot, heat oil over medium flame. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, dried red chillies, and a pinch of hing. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté for 2 minutes. Pour in 1.5 cups water and cook until vegetables are just tender.

4

Combine & Simmer the Sambhar

Add the cooked dal, tamarind water, and sambhar powder to the vegetables. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil. Simmer for 10–12 minutes, adjusting water for your desired consistency. Add salt and taste — the balance of sour, spicy, and savoury is key.

5

Steam the Idlis

Mix salt into the fermented idli batter gently (don't overmix, you'll lose air). Lightly grease the idli moulds with oil. Pour batter into each cavity, about ¾ full. Steam in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without whistle) for 10–12 minutes. A toothpick inserted should come out clean.

6

Serve & Enjoy

Allow idlis to cool for 2 minutes before unmoulding — they release more easily when slightly cooled. Arrange on a plate, ladle hot sambhar over or alongside, and serve with coconut chutney and a small bowl of sambar for dipping.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • Fermenting the batter overnight (8–10 hours) in a warm spot gives the fluffiest idlis.
  • Adding a small piece of jaggery to the sambhar balances the sourness beautifully.
  • Drumstick (moringa) gives sambhar an irreplaceable earthy sweetness — don't skip it if you can find it.
  • Leftover idlis can be pan-fried with a little oil and spices for crispy "kanda poha" style snacks.
