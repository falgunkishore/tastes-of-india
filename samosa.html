Samosa

India's most beloved snack — golden, flaky pastry stuffed with spiced potato perfection.

⏱
Prep Time 30 mins
🔥
Cook Time 20 mins
📊
Difficulty Medium
👤
Makes 12 samosas

Ingredients

  • For the Pastry:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee (for moyan)
  • ½ tsp ajwain (carom seeds)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Cold water as needed
  • For the Filling:
  • 4 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • ½ cup green peas (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ginger, finely grated
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, chopped
  • Oil for deep frying

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Make the Samosa Pastry

In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and ajwain. Add the oil or ghee (this is called "moyan" — it's what makes the pastry flaky). Rub the fat into the flour with your fingertips until it resembles breadcrumbs and a handful holds together when pressed. Add cold water, tablespoon by tablespoon, and knead into a stiff dough — stiffer than roti dough. Cover and rest 20 minutes.

2

Prepare the Spiced Filling

Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add grated ginger and green chilli, sauté 30 seconds. Add peas and cook 2 minutes. Add the mashed potatoes along with all the spices — coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, salt. Mix thoroughly and cook for 2 minutes. Stir in fresh coriander. Cool completely before filling — warm filling will make the pastry soggy.

3

Shape the Cones

Divide the dough into 6 equal balls. Roll each into an oval roughly 6 inches long. Cut the oval in half — each half makes one samosa. Fold a semicircle into a cone shape, sealing the overlapping edge with a little water and pressing firmly. You should have a perfect cone with no gaps.

4

Fill & Seal

Fill each cone generously with the potato filling, leaving about 1cm at the top. Pinch the open top edges together, pressing firmly and creating a pleated seal. Make sure there are absolutely no gaps or the samosa will burst open while frying. Place finished samosas under a damp cloth while you work through the batch.

5

Deep Fry to Perfection

Heat oil in a kadhai or deep pan to 160–165°C — lower than you'd expect. Samosas need to fry low and slow to cook the pastry through without burning the outside. Add 3–4 samosas at a time and fry for 8–10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are an even golden-brown all over. Drain on kitchen paper.

6

Serve with Chutneys

Serve hot samosas with green mint-coriander chutney and tamarind-date chutney. For the classic street-food experience, crush the samosas slightly, drizzle chutneys generously, and top with chopped onions and a squeeze of lemon — this is "samosa chaat" and it is extraordinary.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • The "moyan" fat ratio is crucial — too little fat means hard, tough pastry; right amount means perfect flaky layers.
  • Frying at a lower temperature is the secret to samosas that are cooked through — high heat browns the outside before the inside is done.
  • Samosas can be made ahead and frozen before frying — fry from frozen, adding 2–3 minutes extra.
  • For extra flavour, add 1 tbsp crushed pomegranate seeds (anardana) to the filling.
