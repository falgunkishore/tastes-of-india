Chole Bhature

The heart of Punjabi street food — spiced chickpeas and pillowy fried bread.

⏱
Prep Time 20 mins
🔥
Cook Time 25 mins
📊
Difficulty Medium
👤
Serves 4 people

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp chole masala powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp amchur (dry mango powder)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ½ cup yoghurt
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Pressure Cook the Chickpeas

Drain the soaked chickpeas and transfer them to a pressure cooker. Add enough water to cover by 2 inches and a pinch of salt. Pressure cook for 4–5 whistles on medium heat until soft but not mushy. Reserve the cooking water.

2

Build the Masala Base

Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed kadhai over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add the chopped onions and sauté, stirring often, until deeply golden-brown — about 10–12 minutes. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

3

Add Tomatoes & Spices

Pour in the tomato puree and stir well. Cook on medium heat for 6–8 minutes until the oil separates from the masala. Add chole masala, red chilli powder, and salt. Stir and cook for another 2 minutes.

4

Simmer the Chole

Add the cooked chickpeas along with about 1 cup of reserved cooking water. Stir everything together. Partially cover and simmer on low heat for 15 minutes, allowing the curry to thicken and the chickpeas to absorb the spices. Finish with amchur and garam masala.

5

Make the Bhature Dough

Combine flour, yoghurt, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. Knead into a soft, smooth dough, adding a little warm water if needed. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 30 minutes — this rest is key for puffed bhature.

6

Fry the Bhature

Heat oil in a deep pan to 180°C (350°F). Divide the dough into equal balls and roll each into an oval about 5mm thick. Slide gently into the hot oil and press lightly with the back of a ladle — it will puff up. Fry 1–2 minutes each side until golden. Drain on paper towels.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • Soaking chickpeas overnight gives a much creamier, rounder texture than canned.
  • A tea bag or piece of black cardamom in the pressure cooker gives the chole that dhaba-style dark colour.
  • Resting the bhature dough is non-negotiable — it's what makes them puff perfectly.
  • Serve with sliced onions, green chutney, and a wedge of lemon for the full experience.
