Paneer Butter Masala

Velvety, rich, and deeply aromatic — the jewel of Indian vegetarian cooking.

⏱
Prep Time 10 mins
🔥
Cook Time 25 mins
📊
Difficulty Easy
👤
Serves 4 people

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2 medium onions, roughly chopped
  • 15 raw cashews
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander to garnish
  • 1 bay leaf, 2 cardamom pods

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Make the Tomato-Cashew Paste

Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the bay leaf, cardamom, and onions. Sauté until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook 1–2 minutes. Add tomatoes and cashews. Cover and cook for 10 minutes until tomatoes are completely soft. Cool slightly, then blend to a silky smooth paste. Strain through a sieve for restaurant-quality smoothness.

2

Cook the Masala

Melt butter in the same pan over medium heat. Pour in the blended tomato-cashew paste. Cook on medium-low, stirring frequently, for 8–10 minutes until the paste deepens in colour and the butter begins to separate at the edges.

3

Spice It Up

Add Kashmiri chilli powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder to the cooked paste. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes. The Kashmiri chilli gives the dish its iconic vibrant orange-red colour without excessive heat.

4

Add Cream & Paneer

Pour in ½ cup water and the cream, stirring to create a uniform, luscious gravy. Add sugar and salt. Gently fold in the paneer cubes. Simmer on low heat for 5–6 minutes — just enough for the paneer to absorb the flavours without becoming tough.

5

Finish & Serve

Sprinkle garam masala over the curry and give it a gentle stir. Taste and adjust salt. Turn off the heat and finish with a swirl of cream and a generous handful of freshly chopped coriander. Serve hot with butter naan or steamed basmati rice.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • Soaking paneer in warm water for 15 minutes before adding keeps it soft and pillowy in the gravy.
  • Kashmiri red chilli powder is milder and gives the best colour — don't substitute regular chilli powder.
  • The sugar is essential: it balances the acidity of the tomatoes and is not optional.
  • For a smoky dhaba flavour, place a small piece of charcoal in a bowl, set it on top of the curry, pour a drop of ghee on it, and quickly cover the pan for 2 minutes.
