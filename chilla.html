Besan Ka Chilla

A protein-packed savoury crepe bursting with herbs and spice — ready in under 20 minutes.

⏱
Prep Time 5 mins
🔥
Cook Time 15 mins
📊
Difficulty Easy
👤
Makes 6–8 chillas

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups besan (chickpea flour)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds (or powder)
  • ½ tsp carom seeds (ajwain) — optional
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed (approx. 1 cup)
  • 2 tbsp yoghurt (optional, for fluffiness)
  • Oil for cooking
  • Optional fillings:
  • Grated paneer or cheese
  • Finely chopped bell pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Make the Batter

Sift the besan into a large mixing bowl to remove any lumps. Add turmeric, red chilli powder, cumin, and salt. Gradually add water while whisking continuously until you have a smooth, lump-free batter with the consistency of thin pancake batter — it should coat the back of a spoon lightly but still flow freely. Whisk in yoghurt if using. Rest for 5 minutes.

2

Add the Vegetables

Stir the chopped onion, tomato, green chilli, and fresh coriander into the batter. Add the ajwain seeds if using — they aid digestion and add a delightful subtle flavour. Mix everything well. Taste the raw batter and adjust salt and spices as needed — it should taste seasoned and punchy.

3

Heat the Tawa

Place a non-stick tawa or flat griddle over medium-high heat. Let it get properly hot — hold your palm 5cm above the surface and it should feel very warm within 2 seconds. Drizzle a few drops of oil and spread with a paper towel. The tawa should be well-seasoned with oil before pouring batter.

4

Pour & Spread

Pour a ladleful (about ¼ cup) of batter onto the centre of the hot tawa. Working quickly with the back of the ladle, spread the batter in circular motions outward to form a thin, even circle about 7–8 inches in diameter. Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges. Lower heat slightly to medium.

5

Cook Both Sides

Cook on medium heat for 2–3 minutes until the edges begin to lift and the surface looks dry and set. The underside should be golden-brown with dark spots. Using a thin spatula, carefully flip the chilla and cook the other side for 1–2 minutes. The chilla is done when both sides have golden spots and it moves freely on the pan.

6

Serve Hot

Transfer to a plate and serve immediately — chillas are best eaten fresh off the tawa. Serve with green chutney, yoghurt, or pickle. For a filling meal, spread a thin layer of paneer stuffing on the chilla before folding it in half. Stack with a paper napkin between each to prevent sticking if making a batch.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • A well-seasoned cast-iron or non-stick tawa is essential — batter sticking to the pan is the enemy of a good chilla.
  • Adding a small amount of yoghurt creates a slightly fluffier, softer texture.
  • Don't rush the spreading — if the tawa is too hot, the batter will set before you can spread it thin.
  • For a "moong dal chilla" variation, replace half the besan with soaked and blended green moong dal for even higher protein and a nuttier taste.
