Jalebi

Crispy, golden spirals soaked in fragrant saffron syrup — pure festive bliss.

⏱
Prep + Rest 15 + 15 mins
🔥
Cook Time 15 mins
📊
Difficulty Medium
👤
Makes 20–24 pieces

Ingredients

  • For the Batter:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp cornflour (for crispiness)
  • ½ tsp turmeric (for colour)
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¾ cup warm water (approx.)
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For the Sugar Syrup:
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • ¾ cup water
  • A few strands of saffron
  • ½ tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)
  • ½ tsp lemon juice (to prevent crystallisation)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare the Batter

In a bowl, combine flour, cornflour, turmeric, and baking soda. Gradually add warm water and whisk into a smooth, lump-free batter with the consistency of thin pancake batter — it should flow off a spoon in a steady ribbon. Stir in lemon juice. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.

2

Make the Sugar Syrup

Combine sugar and water in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves. Add saffron, cardamom, and lemon juice. Boil for 5–6 minutes until the syrup reaches 1-string consistency — when you touch a drop between your fingers and pull apart, a single thread forms. Remove from heat and add rose water. Keep warm.

3

Fill the Piping Bottle

Transfer the batter into a squeezable piping bottle or a zip-lock bag with a small corner snipped off. The opening should be about 3–4mm. Practise piping a spiral on a plate before frying — the motion is: spiral outward from the centre, 2–3 full loops, then seal the end.

4

Fry the Jalebis

Heat oil in a wide, flat pan to 175–180°C. The oil should be at least 2 inches deep. Squeeze the batter in circular spiral motions directly into the hot oil — work in batches of 3–4. Fry for 2–3 minutes on each side until the jalebis are crispy and a deep golden-orange. Do not rush — under-fried jalebi will be soft, not crispy.

5

Soak in Syrup

Immediately transfer the hot fried jalebis into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for exactly 1–2 minutes — too short and the syrup won't penetrate, too long and they'll go soggy. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on a wire rack or plate.

6

Serve Immediately

Jalebis are at their absolute best served hot, straight out of the syrup. Plate them and optionally dust with a pinch of saffron or edible silver. Traditionally served alongside rabri (thick condensed milk) or warm milk. The contrast of crispy outside and syrup-soaked interior is the magic.

💡 Chef's Tips

  • The batter should be slightly thick — if it flows too freely, the spirals won't hold their shape.
  • Keep the sugar syrup warm throughout; cold syrup won't coat the jalebis properly.
  • Use a flat, wide pan for even heat distribution and easy spiral formation.
  • For an even crispier texture, add 1 tbsp semolina to the batter.
